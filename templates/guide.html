UNDERSTANDING THE SIGNALS & KPIs
A plain-English guide to every metric in the v2 rotation engine

This engine analyses weekly price and volume data for each ETF and distils it into a single actionable signal. Behind that signal are seven KPI groups — each measuring a different dimension of market behaviour — that together build a picture of where institutional capital is flowing. Large institutions must transact in sizes that leave measurable footprints in the tape: they elevate trading volumes, push prices toward the top or bottom of their weekly range, and generate sustained directional pressure that retail activity cannot replicate. This system is built to detect those footprints.

The v2 model uses a weekly cross-sectional ranking engine. Every metric is ranked against the full universe before being combined into a rotation score — so a reading of 75th-percentile means 75% of the universe scored below this ETF, not an absolute threshold. This makes the model self-calibrating as the universe changes.

Minimum history requirement: 100 weekly observations for full model eligibility. ETFs with fewer than 100 weeks receive a Low confidence score.

OUTPUT

THE SIGNAL

The engine's final verdict — five levels, evaluated in priority order

The signal is a description of current market behaviour for that ETF. It is not a prediction — it tells you what conditions look like at this moment, derived from what large institutional money appears to be doing in the tape right now. Five signals are possible:

STRONG BUY

The highest-conviction signal. Rotation score ≥72, trend ≥3/4, pressure_20w positive, RS20 positive, and confidence ≥50. All primary conditions simultaneously satisfied — the weight of evidence is as strong as this model can produce.

EARLY ACCUMULATION

An inflection-point watch signal. Pressure has just flipped positive after a period of negative readings, RS acceleration is positive, RS4 raw is positive, turnover z-score is positive, and price is still below MA100. Captures the early-stage divergence where institutional buyers begin accumulating before price has recovered. Treat as an alert — structural confirmation is still pending.

ACCUMULATING / HOLD

Rotation score ≥60, trend ≥3/4, pressure_20w positive, RS20 positive. Constructive but not full confirmation. A solid hold signal; watch for the remaining conditions to align.

NEUTRAL

Mixed evidence. No clear institutional directional bias across the primary conditions. Neither accumulation nor distribution is dominant. Hold existing positions and wait for clarity.

EXIT / DISTRIBUTION

Rotation score <35, trend ≤2/4, pressure_20w negative, RS20 negative. Institutional selling is the dominant signal. Review any holding rationale carefully.

Evaluated in priority order — first match wins
PrioritySignalConditions (all must be true)

2 STRONG BUY rotation_score ≥ 72 AND trend_score_raw ≥ 3 AND pressure_20w > 0 AND rs20_raw > 0 AND confidence_score ≥ 50
3 ACCUMULATING/HOLD rotation_score ≥ 60 AND trend_score_raw ≥ 3 AND pressure_20w > 0 AND rs20_raw > 0
4 EXIT/DISTRIBUTION rotation_score < 35 AND trend_score_raw ≤ 2 AND pressure_20w < 0 AND rs20_raw < 0
5 NEUTRAL None of the above conditions met

Note on pressure_prev_20w: The 20-week pressure reading computed using a window ending 5 weeks ago — the "previous state". Early Accumulation checks that this was negative (distribution phase) while the current reading is positive (pressure just turned). The 5-week offset provides a clean before/after comparison.

SCORE

ROTATION SCORE 0 – 100

The primary ranking composite — drives signal classification

The Rotation Score is the main ranking input for signal classification. It combines seven percentile-ranked components into a single number from 0 to 100. All inputs are cross-sectionally ranked — scored relative to the full universe on the same as-of date — so the score captures relative position, not an absolute threshold.

rotation_score = 0.18 × trend_score_pct (trend structure, 0–100) 0.22 × rs20_vol_adj_rank_pct (vol-adjusted RS20, ranked) 0.15 × rs_accel_vol_adj_rank_pct (RS acceleration, ranked) 0.15 × turnover_rank_pct (turnover ratio 20w/100w, ranked) 0.15 × pressure_rank_pct (pressure_20w, ranked) 0.10 × pressure_pos_weeks_pct (% weeks positive pressure) 0.05 × sector_confirmation_pct (0 or 100 based on sector flag)
≥ 72STRONG BUY zone — all components healthy; strong cross-sectional ranking across the universe
60 – 71ACCUMULATING/HOLD zone — constructive but not full alignment
35 – 59Neutral zone — mixed signals; no clear directional bias
< 35EXIT/DISTRIBUTION zone — multiple components in negative territory
SCORE

CONFIDENCE SCORE 0 – 100 · HIGH / MODERATE / LOW

How much to trust the signal — five structural quality dimensions

The Confidence Score measures the reliability of the signal, not its direction. A HIGH confidence STRONG BUY is a different proposition to a LOW confidence one. Five components are weighted:

confidence_score = 30 × min(avg_turn_20w / £5,000,000, 1.0) — liquidity 20 × min(n_obs / 100, 1.0) — history depth (100w target) 15 × max(0, 1 − min(turn_cv20, 1.0)) — turnover stability 20 × (1.0 if sector_confirmed else 0.4) — sector confirmation 15 × mean(required_fields_present) — data completeness
HIGH ≥ 75Strong liquidity (≥£5M/wk), deep history (≥100 weeks), stable turnover, sector confirmed, all fields present
MODERATE 50–74One or more components partially satisfied — exercise appropriate caution
LOW < 50Material deficiency: thin history (<100w), low liquidity, unstable turnover, or sector unconfirmed

The history component targets 100 weekly observations as the full-quality threshold. ETFs with fewer than 21 weeks cannot compute RS20 at all. ETFs with fewer than 100 weeks receive a proportionally penalised history component.

KPI 1

TREND SCORE 0 – 4

Are the moving averages pointing in the right direction?

Trend Score checks four structural conditions on the price and its moving averages. The MA20 (20-week moving average) captures the short-to-medium term trend; the MA100 (100-week moving average) captures the long-term structural trend. Using MA100 rather than MA120 aligns the long-term lookback with the model's minimum history requirement.

1Close is above MA20 — the ETF is beating its own recent average; short-term momentum positive
2Close is above MA100 — trading above the long-term structural average; major trend intact
3MA20 above MA100 — short-term trend stronger than long-term; bullish moving average alignment
4MA100 now > MA100 twenty weeks ago — even the long-term average is accelerating upward; slow money confirmed
4/4Fully aligned uptrend — all conditions green
3/4Mostly constructive — one lagging condition, trend broadly intact
2/4Mixed — trend not established in either direction
1/4Deteriorating — most structural conditions turned negative
0/4Full downtrend — all four conditions negative
trend_score_pct = 25 × trend_score_raw (normalises 0–4 to 0–100 for rotation score)
KPI 2

PRESSURE 20W signed £ value

Which direction is institutional capital flowing?

Pressure 20W is the 20-week cumulative sum of signed dollar pressure — the weekly CLV (Close Location Value) multiplied by pound turnover. CLV measures where the weekly close falls within the high-low range: +1.0 means the ETF closed at the very top of its weekly range (maximum buying pressure), −1.0 means it closed at the very bottom (maximum selling pressure). Multiplying by turnover weights the signal by capital deployed.

CLV = ((close − low) − (high − close)) / (high − low) dv = close_GBP × volume (prices normalised from GBp to GBP) pressure_week = CLV × dv pressure_20w = sum of pressure_week over last 20 weeks

Unlike the v1 CRDP20, which was a simple mean of CLV×DV, v2 also computes:

▸pressure_ratio_20w — up-pressure / down-pressure over 20 weeks. High ratio = asymmetric buying.
▸pressure_pos_weeks_pct — % of the last 20 weeks where pressure was positive. 100% = accumulation every week.
▸pressure_all_positive_20w — flag: 1 if no single week showed negative pressure over the 20w window.

The sign of pressure_20w is more important than its magnitude — positive means net institutional buying over the period; negative means net selling (distribution).

KPI 3

RAW RELATIVE STRENGTH vs VWRP.L benchmark

Is this ETF beating the global market?

Raw Relative Strength (RS) measures the ETF's return over a period minus the benchmark's (VWRP.L — Vanguard FTSE All-World) return over the same period. A positive reading means the ETF is outperforming the global market; negative means it is lagging. Three horizons are computed:

rs4_raw = (close / close[4w ago] − 1) − (benchmark / benchmark[4w ago] − 1) rs12_raw = (close / close[12w ago] − 1) − (benchmark / benchmark[12w ago] − 1) rs20_raw = (close / close[20w ago] − 1) − (benchmark / benchmark[20w ago] − 1) rs_accel_raw = rs4_raw − rs12_raw (is short-term RS faster than medium-term?)

RS acceleration (rs_accel_raw) is a momentum-of-momentum measure. A positive value means the ETF is outperforming the benchmark more over the recent 4 weeks than over the previous 12 — momentum is building. A negative value means relative strength is decelerating.

RS20 > +10%Strongly outperforming — capital actively rotating into this ETF vs global market
+3% to +10%Meaningful outperformance — consistent with sector rotation inflows
−3% to +3%Broadly tracking the global market — no significant edge
−10% to −3%Underperforming — capital may be rotating away
< −10%Significantly underperforming — strong relative weakness vs benchmark
KPI 4

VOLATILITY-ADJUSTED RELATIVE STRENGTH ranked 0–100pct

Efficient outperformance — filtering out high-volatility noise

A v2 upgrade over raw RS. Vol-adjusted RS divides each raw RS reading by the ETF's 20-week realised volatility — its typical weekly return fluctuation. This prevents high-volatility sleeves (e.g. single-country emerging market ETFs) from dominating raw RS rankings through noise alone. An ETF with RS20 of +8% but volatility of 5%/week is less impressive than one with RS20 of +4% and volatility of 0.8%/week.

vol_20w = rolling 20-week std dev of weekly returns (ddof=0) rs20_vol_adj = rs20_raw / vol_20w rs_accel_vol_adj = rs_accel_raw / vol_20w rs4_vol_adj = rs4_raw / vol_20w rs12_vol_adj = rs12_raw / vol_20w — If vol_20w is zero or null, all vol-adj fields are set to null —

These raw vol-adj values are then winsorised (capped at 2nd/98th percentile across the universe) and cross-sectionally ranked 0–100. A rank of 80 means this ETF has higher vol-adj RS than 80% of the universe. These ranks feed the rotation score; the raw values are retained for display and interpretability.

≥ 80pctTop quintile — efficient outperformance, not just high volatility masquerading as RS
60–79pctAbove median — constructive relative leadership on risk-adjusted basis
40–59pctAround median — no strong edge in either direction
20–39pctBelow median — vol-adjusted lagging
< 20pctBottom quintile — significant vol-adjusted underperformance
KPI 5

TURNOVER BLOCK ratio · z-score · stability

Is more institutional money moving than usual?

All turnover metrics use £ turnover (close in GBP × volume), not unit volume. Prices stored in GBp are divided by 100 before computing turnover. Three complementary measures:

avg_turn_20w = rolling 20-week mean of (close_GBP × volume) avg_turn_100w = rolling 100-week mean of (close_GBP × volume) turnover_ratio_20_100 = avg_turn_20w / avg_turn_100w turnover_std_100w = rolling 100-week std dev (ddof=0) turnover_z_20 = (turn_latest − avg_turn_100w) / turnover_std_100w turn_cv20 = 20-week std dev / avg_turn_20w (coefficient of variation — choppiness)
▸Turnover ratio — is recent (20w) participation above the long-term (100w) baseline? Above 1.0 = expanding institutional interest. Cross-sectionally ranked for the rotation score.
▸Turnover z-score — is this specific week anomalous vs the 100w history? A large positive z-score suggests a catalyst event or institutional block trade. Winsorised before ranking.
▸Turnover CV (stability) — a high coefficient of variation means choppy, inconsistent volume. High instability penalises the confidence score via the stability component (max(0, 1 − min(cv20, 1.0))).
≥£5M/wk avgInstitutional grade — sufficient depth to absorb large transactions; signals highly reliable
£1M–£5M/wkModerate liquidity — meaningful signals, some week-to-week noise
£250K–£1M/wkLower liquidity — signals noisier; treat with caution
<£250K/wkLow liquidity — all signals unreliable; confidence will be LOW
KPI 6

SECTOR BREADTH & CONFIRMATION

Is the whole sector participating — or is this a lonely outlier?

Sector breadth checks whether the ETF's signal is confirmed by the behaviour of its sector peers. A STRONG BUY in an ETF that is the only outperformer in its sector carries less weight than one where most peers are also above benchmark. Three categories of breadth metrics are computed for each sector:

ABenchmark-relative breadth (primary): % of sector ETFs with RS4, RS12, RS20, and RS accel positive — i.e. beating the global market. These drive the sector confirmation flag.
BPressure breadth (primary): % of sector ETFs with positive pressure_20w — net accumulation. Also drives sector confirmation.
CInternal structure breadth (secondary): % of sector ETFs above MA20 and MA100. Confirmation context, not primary.
sector_confirmed = 1 if: sector_count ≥ 3 (minimum sector size) AND sector_pct_rs12_pos ≥ 60% AND sector_pct_rs20_pos ≥ 50% AND sector_pct_positive_pressure ≥ 60%

If sector_count < 3, sector_confirmed = 0 and the confidence score's sector component is penalised (0.4 instead of 1.0). The sector_confirmation_pct in the rotation score is 100 if confirmed, 0 if not.

PRACTICE

READING SIGNALS TOGETHER

How to use the Dashboard, Heatmap, and History in combination

The Dashboard is the primary view for weekly review. The rotation score, confidence bucket, and signal reason are the first things to check. For ETFs in EARLY ACCUMULATION, the detail panel shows whether the pressure flip is accompanied by expanding turnover and positive RS acceleration — if all three align, it is a higher-conviction early signal.

The Heatmap is the most efficient tool for reading the full universe at a glance. Sort by RS VOL-ADJ rank or ROTATION to surface the strongest ETFs immediately. The pension fund filter allows you to restrict the view to only the ETFs mapped to a specific fund — useful for checking which proxy ETFs are strongest within a given pension fund's investment universe.

The History page shows signal transitions. Recency matters: a STRONG BUY that has just triggered carries different risk/reward to one that has been stable for 12 weeks. An ETF that has moved from NEUTRAL → ACCUMULATING/HOLD → STRONG BUY over three consecutive weeks is a more compelling case than a single-week jump.

For confidence interpretation: always cross-check the confidence bucket before acting on a signal. A HIGH confidence STRONG BUY in a fully liquid ETF with 100+ weeks of history and confirmed sector breadth is the system's best-quality signal. A LOW confidence STRONG BUY should be treated as preliminary — perhaps history is building or sector size is thin — and monitored before acting.
